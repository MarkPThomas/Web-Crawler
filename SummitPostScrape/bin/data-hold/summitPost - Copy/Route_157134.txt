<a name="chapter_1"></a><h2 id="first_h2">Approach</h2>
<br>
Use the information provided in the <a href="http://www.summitpost.com/mountains/display_component.pl?type=component&amp;mountain_id=1396&amp;route_id=&amp;component_id=14290&amp;object_id=14290">main page</a> to get to the "Mount Olympus Trailhead".<br>
<a name="chapter_2"></a><h2>Route Description</h2>
<br>
Simply follow the trail.  The first 1400 feet of ascent is a steep, but not troublesome, climb along the north side of Tolcate Canyon.  After crossing Tolcate Creek, however, the trail steepens considerably.  Climb another 2000 feet over merely 1.5 miles to Olympus's South shoulder and take in excellent views to the south and east. Most of the remaining 600 feet to the summit is gained by scrambling over straightforward class 3 terrain.  Warning, the smooth quartzite rock that you'll encounter tends to be a bit slick when dry, murderously slippery when wet.<br>
<br>
This <a href="http://www.topozone.com/map.asp?z=12&amp;n=4500275&amp;e=433375&amp;s=50&amp;size=l&amp;u=0&amp;layer=DRG25">topographic map</a> nicely illustrates the route.  The USGS 7.5-minute quad is named "Sugar House".<br>
<br>
PellucidWombat describes an interesting <a href="http://www.summitpost.org/mountains/display_component.pl?type=component&amp;mountain_id=1396&amp;route_id=1055&amp;component_id=14296&amp;object_id=14296">easy 5th class variation</a> that provides an alternative to the "Blister Hill" portion of the Mount Olympus Trail.<br>

<a name="chapter_3"></a><h2>Essential Gear</h2>
<br>
Standard hiking gear.  In the late summer months, no water is found along the route.  However, as the route is so short (3.5 miles one way to the summit), you needn't worry too much.  Rock shoes are unnecessary, though your footwear should grip well and have enough stability for the punishing, rocky descent.<br>

(There is another ridge further south, but I stayed on the northernmost one)<br><br><br><br><br><br>Apart from a few short detours to the side to avoid some roofs and +5.7 stuff, I was able to stay on the arete. The climbing was mostly 3rd-4th class, with occasional 5th class moves. <br><br><br><br><br><br>While there are definitely places that you don't want to fall, the exposure isn't too bad and you have nice views of the valley &amp; the Mount Olympus Trail below.<br><br><br><br><br><br>Near the end one is confronted with some exposed face climbing with small handholds on the north side of the arete, but there is enough in the way of a faint ledge system that you can mostly edge up this part. After the face climbing, the ridge soon peters out and a short walk takes you back to the main trail.</td>
