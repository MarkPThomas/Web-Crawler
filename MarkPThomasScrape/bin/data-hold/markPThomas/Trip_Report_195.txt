<h1>
<a name="TOC-Washer-Woman-In-Search-of-Suds-III-5.10-C0-or-5.11--5-6P-"></a>Washer Woman, In Search of Suds (III, 5.10+ C0 or 5.11-, 5-6P)</h1>
<h2>
<a name="TOC-November-13-15-2012"></a>November 13-15, 2012</h2>
<br>

Dirk and I drove a long ways out on the White Rim Trail to climb a wacky, semi-remote tower called Washer Woman (3 hrs of dirt road driving in a high clearance, 4WD truck). The drive was beautiful and the climbing was great and in a spectacular setting. Dirk managed to lead the 5.11- final pitch clean while I yarded on a few bolts to get through. The summit register had some very interesting entries with some famous climbers signing in. Finishing with a rappel through the arch in the tower was a unique way to end a desert climb.

Unfortunately it took us longer than expected to make the drive out here, and the days were very short this time of year, so we decided to relax, hike around, and head up to the tower early the next morning.<br>

						So after some lounging we headed up a river wash and ridge to the south to get some nice views of the formations. It was nice being back in this area, as I had mountain biked out to this very spot when I was in high school. Although I had no idea what these formations were, looking back through old photos I found that I had indeed photographed Washer Woman some 12 years ago.
<br>

As we headed up the ridge, Dirk and admired some of the interesting rock features in the area, like the sandstone imprints of riverbed textures.<br>

<br>
<center>
<a href="https://lh5.googleusercontent.com/-6dAAOnLXJ4c/ULJ_eUUdehI/AAAAAAABkI8/iH7i177IcU4/s1600/2012-11-15%252520-%25252075%252520-%252520Dirk%252520after%252520Rapp%2525201.jpg"><img src="https://lh5.googleusercontent.com/-6dAAOnLXJ4c/ULJ_eUUdehI/AAAAAAABkI8/iH7i177IcU4/s800/2012-11-15%252520-%25252075%252520-%252520Dirk%252520after%252520Rapp%2525201.jpg" style="border-width:0px;background-color:transparent"></a>
<p><b><i>Dirk after rappel 1. The rappel 2 anchors are very exposed and awkward to access and can be seen below (blue tat).</i></b></p>
</center>
						The first rappel is very exposed and awkward to get to. Basically you have to lower off tat attached to bolts on the ridge to get to a microledge and bolts out on the face. From this semi-hanging anchor one then sets up to rappel the arch.<br>
<br>
<center>
<a href="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/-lmCuVet4YcM/ULJ_f_JZBiI/AAAAAAABkJA/JPai6ijppZA/s1600/2012-11-15%252520-%25252075a%252520-%252520Rappelling%252520the%252520arch%252520-%252520by%252520Dirk%252520Summers.jpg"><img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/-lmCuVet4YcM/ULJ_f_JZBiI/AAAAAAABkJA/JPai6ijppZA/s800/2012-11-15%252520-%25252075a%252520-%252520Rappelling%252520the%252520arch%252520-%252520by%252520Dirk%252520Summers.jpg" style="border-width:0px;background-color:transparent"></a>
<p><b><i>Rappel 2, Dirk rappelling the arch! (by Dirk Summers)</i></b></p>
</center>
<br>
<br>
<center>
<a href="https://lh6.googleusercontent.com/-fUPQ18hJpvc/ULJ_hMyfZtI/AAAAAAABkJE/7IxZbnPDWt8/s1600/2012-11-15%252520-%25252075b%252520-%252520Rappelling%252520the%252520arch%252520framing%252520panorama%252520-%252520by%252520Dirk%252520Summers.jpg"><img src="https://lh6.googleusercontent.com/-fUPQ18hJpvc/ULJ_hMyfZtI/AAAAAAABkJE/7IxZbnPDWt8/s800/2012-11-15%252520-%25252075b%252520-%252520Rappelling%252520the%252520arch%252520framing%252520panorama%252520-%252520by%252520Dirk%252520Summers.jpg" style="border-width:0px;background-color:transparent"></a>
<p><b><i>Rappel 2, arch profile on rappel. (by Dirk Summers)</i></b></p>
</center>
<br>
<br>
<center>
<a href="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/-srIoZZA8PXA/ULJ_z-xNsII/AAAAAAABkJQ/osyP49g3dsE/s1600/2012-11-15%252520-%25252075e%252520-%252520Mark%252520rappelling%252520the%252520arch%252520-%252520by%252520Dirk%252520Summers.jpg"><img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/-srIoZZA8PXA/ULJ_z-xNsII/AAAAAAABkJQ/osyP49g3dsE/s800/2012-11-15%252520-%25252075e%252520-%252520Mark%252520rappelling%252520the%252520arch%252520-%252520by%252520Dirk%252520Summers.jpg" style="border-width:0px;background-color:transparent"></a>
<p><b><i>Rappel 2, rappelling the arch! (by Dirk Summers)</i></b></p>
</center>
						I have done a number of long free hanging rappels, but rappelling an arch feels very different being out in space.&Acirc;&nbsp;<div>
<br>
<center>
<a href="https://lh4.googleusercontent.com/-MGhdYpbHoTA/ULJ_3tvrQDI/AAAAAAABkJo/P7vqn0BVVAU/s1600/2012-11-15%252520-%25252076%252520-%252520After%252520rapp%2525202%252520through%252520the%252520arch.jpg"><img src="https://lh4.googleusercontent.com/-MGhdYpbHoTA/ULJ_3tvrQDI/AAAAAAABkJo/P7vqn0BVVAU/s800/2012-11-15%252520-%25252076%252520-%252520After%252520rapp%2525202%252520through%252520the%252520arch.jpg" style="border-width:0px;background-color:transparent"></a>
<p><b><i>Rappel 2, after rappelling the arch!</i></b></p>
</center>
<br>

From here we had 3 more short rappels to the ground. Don't do it in 2 or else your rope will get stuck. Ours got stuck on 2 of the rappels, and with a bit of climbing back up we managed to free it We also carried out a new 60m rope that was stuck and abandoned on the last rappel.<br>
<br>
<center>
<a href="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/-YjXAD0IJSPU/ULJ_48CJrBI/AAAAAAABkJs/tAdPmN__cQM/s1600/2012-11-15%252520-%25252077%252520-%252520Rapp%2525203%252520anchor.jpg"><img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/-YjXAD0IJSPU/ULJ_48CJrBI/AAAAAAABkJs/tAdPmN__cQM/s800/2012-11-15%252520-%25252077%252520-%252520Rapp%2525203%252520anchor.jpg" style="border-width:0px;background-color:transparent"></a>
<p><b><i>Rappel 3 anchor.</i></b></p>
</center>
<br>
<br>
<center>
<a href="https://lh6.googleusercontent.com/-p6MZ0AZZpHc/ULJ_7z2MKoI/AAAAAAABkJ4/xGq0QJ9wOv0/s1600/2012-11-15%252520-%25252079%252520-%252520Dirk%252520freeing%252520stuck%252520rope%252520on%252520rapp%2525203.jpg"><img src="https://lh6.googleusercontent.com/-p6MZ0AZZpHc/ULJ_7z2MKoI/AAAAAAABkJ4/xGq0QJ9wOv0/s800/2012-11-15%252520-%25252079%252520-%252520Dirk%252520freeing%252520stuck%252520rope%252520on%252520rapp%2525203.jpg" style="border-width:0px;background-color:transparent"></a>
<p><b><i>Dirk freeing the stuck rope on rappel 3.</i></b></p>
</center>
<br>
<br>
<center>
<a href="https://lh4.googleusercontent.com/-dfvJzsqmyZg/UPUmNOjSA7I/AAAAAAABoaE/3vi9lI1qJPs/s1600/2012-11-14%252520-%25252034a%252520-%252520Washer%252520Woman%252520Tower%252520-%252520by%252520Dirk%252520Summers%252520-%252520annotate.jpg"><img src="https://lh4.googleusercontent.com/-dfvJzsqmyZg/UPUmNOjSA7I/AAAAAAABoaE/3vi9lI1qJPs/s800/2012-11-14%252520-%25252034a%252520-%252520Washer%252520Woman%252520Tower%252520-%252520by%252520Dirk%252520Summers%252520-%252520annotate.jpg" style="border-width:0px;background-color:transparent"></a>
<p><b><i>Washer Woman seen from the West. (by Dirk Summers)</i></b></p>
</center>
<br>

While Washer Woman takes some time to get to, it is a great desert tower in a beautiful setting, containing some very nice climbing! I think I like it more than Castleton Tower. Hopefully next time I can spend more time on the White Rim trail to do a lot more of these towers we saw on the drive in! Maybe Monster Tower next time . . .
