<a name="chapter_1"></a><h2 id="first_h2">Overview</h2>This relatively unkown peak certainly deserves more attention.   John Veranth's <i>"Hiking the Wasatch"</i> only mentions the peak as part of the Bullion Divide ridgerun. <br>
<br>
Red Baldy sits on the Alpine Ridge seperating Little Cottonwood and American Fork Canyons in the <a href="http://www.fs.fed.us/r4/wcnf/">Wasatch-Cache National Forest</a>.   At 11,171 feet it is among the highest peaks in the Wasatch, but it sits between the higher <a href="http://www.summitpost.org/show/mountain_link.pl/mountain_id/2103">American Fork Twin Peaks</a> and <a href="http://www.summitpost.org/show/mountain_link.pl/mountain_id/2209">White Baldy</a>, thus, it sees relatively few ascents. <br>
<br>
Red Baldy is most commonly climbed from White Pine Canyon in Little Cottonwood via a number of routes, but it can also be climbed from the south by hiking to Silver Glance Lake, then routefinding up to "West Pass" between Red and White Baldy, and finishing via the west ridge.  Red Baldy is also one of multiple summits often climbed by advanced hikers as part of the <a href="http://www.summitpost.org/show/route_link.pl/route_id/4803/object_id/3428">Bullion Divide</a> ridgerun.<br>

<a name="chapter_2"></a><h2>Getting There</h2>
<b>Trailhead:</b>  <font color="blue"><b>White Pine</b></font>  - <b>X,XXX'</b>  <br>
Take the 6200 South exit (exit #6) off I-215 and follow highway 190 south-east approximately 2 miles to the intersection with Big Cottonwood Canyon road. Continue straight through the stoplight and follow the road another 4 miles to the mouth of Little Cottonwood Canyon, where an electronic billboard on the right provides current road and/or weather conditions. <br>
<br>
Drive about 2/3 of the way up Little Cottonwood Canyon, approximately 30 min from Salt Lake City. There are signs marking the turnoff on the south side of the road for the trailhead parking lot. The turnoff is just past the turnoff for the Tanner's Flat campground.<br>
<br>
<b>Neighboring Peaks:</b> This peak is also often approached via <a href="http://www.summitpost.org/show/mountain_link.pl/mountain_id/4093">Red Top</a> and <a href="http://www.summitpost.org/show/mountain_link.pl/mountain_id/2209">White Baldy</a>, .<br>

<a name="chapter_4"></a><h2>Red Tape</h2>Red Baldy is located within the Wasatch-Cache National Forest as well as the Salt Lake County Watershed.<br>
<br>
<u>Camping</u><br>
<ul>
<li> Anywhere 100 ft off of trails<br>
</li>
<li> Anywhere at least 200 ft away from streams and lakes. <br>
</li>
</ul>
<br>
<u>Human Waste</u><br>
<ul><li>Please attend to 'business' at least 200 ft away from any water source.  <br>
</li></ul>
<u> Other Restrictions</u><br>
<ul><li>Also, no swimming is allowed in the reservoirs.<br>
</li></ul>
