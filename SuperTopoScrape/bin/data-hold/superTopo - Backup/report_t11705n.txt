<b><u>September 6, 2012</u></b><br>
<b><u><i>(Carryout Day 2)</i></u></b><br>
<br>
As our Teton extravaganza was coming to an end, we made sure to soak up as much of this incredible place as we could. We woke early to catch an amazing sunrise before packing up camp to head out.<br>

<b><i>Up and Over Mt. Teewinot</i></b><br>
<br>
The scramble up the remained of the SW Couloir route was straightforward, and we left our gear just after passing through the large notch to pick up on the descent. The remainder of the Southwest Chute route from the eastern plateau is just a small bit of class 3-4 scrambling (mostly class 3).<br>

<b><u>Summit Fun</u></b><br>
<br>
The summit area of Teewinot was really cool, and we spent a long time playing around on the different rock towers up there before finally descending the East Face. The Teewinot summit has got to be one of the best summit photo ops anywhere.<br>

<b><u>Descent</u></b><br>
<br>
<a href="https://picasaweb.google.com/lh/photo/9ZA2SqINc7BORgaaf_tgitMTjNZETYmyPJy0liipFm0?feat=directlink" target="_blank" rel="nofollow"><img src="https://lh6.googleusercontent.com/-smn7PPPe_CM/UFQjGSDw48I/AAAAAAABOok/0rSwl25KQyE/s800/2012-08-27%2520-%252011%2520-%2520Teewinot%2520-%2520annotated.jpg"></a><br>
<b><i>Mt. Teewinot's East Face, which we used as a descent route</i></b><br>
<br>
The East Face isn’t a terribly interesting route and it mostly endless class 2 traversing on ledges with the occasional class 3 downclimb, and it is a bit loose in places (but nowhere near as loose as much of the terrain we had climbed on in the days prior!). The class 4 cruxes required some thought to downclimb securely, but we felt no need to use the rappel stations, even climbing in approach shoes with our large packs.<br>
<br>
<a href="https://picasaweb.google.com/105894936663116565516/20120906TetonsWStephTeewinotSWCouloirIICl4EFaceDescentIICl4#5787575445047452626" target="_blank" rel="nofollow"><img src="http://lh5.ggpht.com/-lgSJ5WB4mz0/UFGZNBDTo9I/AAAAAAABSok/x4Imran8GkA/s800/2012-09-06%252520-%25252038%252520-%252520Long%252520ways%252520down%252520the%252520E%252520Face.jpg"></a><br>
<b><i>6,000 ft descent. Woohoo!</i></b><br>
<br>
<a href="https://picasaweb.google.com/105894936663116565516/20120906TetonsWStephTeewinotSWCouloirIICl4EFaceDescentIICl4#5789756875544136898" target="_blank" rel="nofollow"><img src="http://lh4.ggpht.com/-SxHENgViV6E/UFlZM-6vDMI/AAAAAAABSoo/TbhpAKlHJI4/s800/2012-09-06%252520-%25252038a%252520-%252520Downclimbing%252520the%252520crux%252520-%252520Steph.JPG"></a><br>
<b><i>Downclimbing the class 4 crux. Probably would have felt chill if not for the pack. (by Steph Abegg)</i></b><br>
<br>
Eventually we hit the very well worn climbers trail and descended the endless switchbacks down to the cars.<br>
<br>
<a href="https://picasaweb.google.com/105894936663116565516/20120906TetonsWStephTeewinotSWCouloirIICl4EFaceDescentIICl4#5787575465728036482" target="_blank" rel="nofollow"><img src="http://lh6.ggpht.com/-IeAb_Pg6pKc/UFGZOOF8ToI/AAAAAAABSos/-SNBSIMtdZM/s800/2012-09-06%252520-%25252039%252520-%252520E%252520Face%252520of%252520Teewinot.jpg"></a><br>
<b><i>East Face of Teewinot seen from the end of the scrambling and the start of the nice climber trail.</i></b><br>
<br>
We arrived at the cars just in time. Despite having a second day in a row of nice, sunny weather, the morning sun had been covered by clouds as we descended Mt. Teewinot. Just as we got back to the cars at the Lupine Meadows trailhead, it started to rain.<br>
<br>
From there I headed into Jackson Hole for a burger and beer while Steph disappeared (as is her custom), with plans to reunite for one last climb the following morning.<br>
